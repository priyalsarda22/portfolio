Parkinson's Disease Diagnostic Tool

Addressing gender disparities in Parkinson's diagnosis through an alternative diagnostic device designed for females over 60

Medical Device Design User-Centered Design Healthcare Equity Wearable Technology
Back to Projects

Problem Statement

Females over the age of 60 need an alternative diagnostic tool for Parkinson's disease because of gender differences in motor and psychological presentation during early phases of the disease.

Background

Women are at significant risk for experiencing disparities in Parkinson's disease diagnosis and treatment:

  • Different prodromal symptoms - Women present with different early warning signs than men
  • Less likely to show tremors during early phases, leading to missed diagnoses
  • Misdiagnosis is common - Symptoms often attributed to other conditions like carpal tunnel
  • Treatment delays - Women statistically start physical treatment 8 months after men
  • Less likely to receive Deep Brain Stimulation despite having better outcomes
  • Caregiver burden - Often prioritize others' health over their own

User Story: Hattie

To understand the real-world impact of diagnostic disparities, we developed a user persona based on research:

2010
Introduction
Full-time caregiver for husband. Sees a therapist regularly for depression who recommends knitting as a hobby.
2013
Symptoms Start
Notices a slight tremor in hand as she picks up her granddaughter.
2014
Seeking Help
Friends notice tremors and recommend she sees a doctor. Her main support system is this group of friends.
2014
Misdiagnosis
Doctor assumes tremors are from carpal tunnel syndrome. She has fewer physical symptoms and more non-motor symptoms (fatigue, sleep disturbances) that she doesn't connect to PD.
2016
Late Diagnosis
After two years of worsening symptoms, she returns to the doctor and finally receives a Parkinson's diagnosis.
2018
Treatment Begins
Starts physical treatment. Statistics show women start PD physical treatment 8 months after men on average.
2020
Denied DBS
Her depressive state leads doctors to deny her the Deep Brain Stimulation procedure, even though women typically have better outcomes with DBS.

Design Requirements

Critical Requirements

  • Equal performance across genders
  • Cloud system and storage
  • Automatically calculates metrics

Essential Requirements

  • Within +/-5% of current industry standards
  • Less than 0.25 kg weight
  • 1 year of daily use durability
  • Meets FDA safety standards

Desired Features

  • Total cost less than $100
  • Less than 5 steps, takes less than 5 minutes
  • Manually-inputted information is qualitative

Design Constraints

1. Simple User Interface

  • Elder-friendly design with readable fonts
  • Minimum usage of app interfaces
  • Clear instructions and all-inclusive accessibility

2. Take-Home Device

  • Accommodating to elderly patients
  • Minimizes pressure of clinical visits
  • Enables larger data sets for better diagnostic results

3. Discreet Design

  • Does not make it obvious patient has a condition
  • Not bulky, fits under normal daily clothing
  • Neutral color palette

4. Non-Interfering with Daily Activities

  • Can withstand normal physical motion and pressures
  • Comfortable for extended wear
  • Compatible with activities like knitting

Take-home monitoring enables comfort and more data

Device must not interfere with daily activities

Value Proposition

Thorough

Observes many facets of symptoms and collects enough data for better clinical judgements and earlier diagnosis

Easy to Use

No confusion for patient, caregiver, therapist, or physician. Minimal resources, time, and cost required

Accurate

Quantitative results combat subjective assessment issues in PD diagnosis

Better Outcomes

Improves outcomes for women by quantifying symptoms and catching Parkinson's sooner

Project Context

This project was completed as part of BMED 2250 (Biomedical Engineering Design) as a Phase 1 problem definition and user research exercise. The work involved:

  • Literature review on gender disparities in Parkinson's diagnosis
  • User persona development based on research findings
  • Requirements engineering and constraint analysis
  • Value proposition development

This foundational work sets the stage for subsequent design phases including concept generation, prototyping, and validation.
