Project Overview

I worked with a team of 4 to develop a wearable diagnostic device addressing the gender disparity in Parkinson's Disease detection. We learned that females over 60 present with different motor and psychological symptoms than traditional diagnostic criteria account for, leading to delayed diagnosis and treatment. That's why we decided to create an alternative diagnostic tool specifically designed to detect these different symptom patterns.

Our Mission: Create an accessible, accurate diagnostic tool for Parkinson's Disease that accounts for gender differences in motor and psychological presentation during early phases of the disease.

Clinical Gap Analysis

  • Diagnostic Disparity: Women present with different prodromal symptoms (fatigue, depression, sleep disturbances) vs. classic tremors
  • Misdiagnosis Rate: Often misdiagnosed as carpal tunnel syndrome, essential tremor, or age-related changes
  • Treatment Delay: Women statistically start PD physical treatment 8 months after men
  • Access to Care: Less likely to be recommended for Deep Brain Stimulation despite having better outcomes

Design Requirements

Concept Development

We developed multiple concepts to address the diagnostic gap. I helped evaluate each design using a weighted Pugh matrix across manufacturing feasibility, usability, and functionality criteria. We selected two concepts to prototype and test.

Concepts Developed

Concept 1: Digital Stylus 3D-printed stylus with embedded accelerometer for spiral drawing test quantification
Physical prototype of the digital stylus concept with integrated accelerometer
Concept 2: Wrist Bracelet Wrist-worn accelerometer bracelet for arm swing and tremor detection during daily activities
3D-printed prototype of wrist bracelet with adjustable Velcro strap

Design Selection: We chose the wrist bracelet concept because it scored highest in our evaluation with superior portability, multi-symptom detection capability, and lower manufacturing cost. This design allows continuous monitoring during natural movement patterns.

Signal Processing

Signal Processing Pipeline

Complete signal processing pipeline from raw acceleration data to symptom detection

Key Metrics Calculated

  • Cadence (steps/min): Derived from autocorrelation analysis - changes indicate bradykinesia progression
  • Step Symmetry (0-1): Calculated from autocorrelation peaks - declining values indicate uneven gait
  • Frequency Band Areas: Computed via Fourier Transformation
    • Low frequency (0-5 Hz): Normal gait patterns
    • Medium frequency (5-10 Hz): Postural tremor detection
    • High frequency (10-25 Hz): Resting tremor identification
  • Temporal Trend Analysis: Metrics tracked over time to detect symptom progression

Model Validation Results

Healthy Control (20 trials)

  • Rotational tremor: Stable
  • Cadence change: 0.7% (minimal)
  • Stride regularity: 0.2% change
  • Coefficient of variation: <5%

Simulated PD (10 trials)

  • Rotational tremor: Increased
  • Cadence change: 7% (elevated variability)
  • Stride regularity: -13.7% decrease
  • Successfully detected symptom changes
